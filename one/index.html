Protests over plan to close Riverdale rec centre

by Laura Fraser
Residents gathered outside the centre on Saturday morning.

Dozens of residents gathered outside the Riverdale community recreation centre on Saturday, protesting a city proposal that would shutter the 41-year-old facility next spring. The building houses the only public pool, after-school program and seniors' lunch in the east-end neighbourhood.

City staff say the roof and mechanical systems are at the end of their life and that repairs would cost an estimated $14 million. Councillors are due to vote on the closure in November, and have asked residents to weigh in before then.
