City unveils a plan for protected bike lanes downtown — and wants to know where they should go

DO
By Dana Okereke
Mar 14, 2025 · 6 min read
[ Photo: Telegraph Avenue looking north — The Oaklandside ]

Oakland's public works department released a draft network of protected bike lanes this week, threading roughly four miles of physically separated lanes through downtown and out to West Oakland. City planners say the routes are a starting point — not a final map.

"Where a lane goes matters as much as whether it exists," said transportation director Lena Cho. "The people who ride these streets every day know things our traffic counts don't."

The draft prioritizes the Telegraph Avenue corridor and a contested crossing at 20th, where right-turning buses and the weekend Uptown crowd regularly mix with cyclists. A western spur would, for the first time, connect West Oakland to the downtown grid.

Public works will hold three open houses in April before bringing a revised map to the city council in May.
